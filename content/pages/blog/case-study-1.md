Some weddings do not need scale to feel full. This one began at city hall with only immediate family present, a short list of portraits, and an afternoon that stayed open on purpose.

That open space changed the whole tone of the coverage. We were able to move slowly, wait for the light to settle against the stone buildings, and photograph the pair as they actually were with each other instead of rushing from one checkpoint to the next.

The final gallery leaned into texture, winter color, and those in-between pauses that usually disappear in a tighter timeline. It is one of my favorite reminders that intimacy photographs beautifully when it is given enough room.
