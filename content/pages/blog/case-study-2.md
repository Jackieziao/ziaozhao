The brief was simple: make the brand feel more tactile, more human, and easier to place across the website, press requests, and social launches. That meant we needed more than a few polished portraits.

We planned the day around variety in pace and framing. Wide scenes established the environment, tighter crops caught materials and gestures, and a sequence of lightly directed portraits gave the founder images that felt elevated but still approachable.

By the end of the session, the client had a visual library rather than a single campaign set. That is usually the difference between photographs that look good for a week and photographs that keep working for a brand all year.
