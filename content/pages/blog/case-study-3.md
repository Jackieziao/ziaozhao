This session worked because it never tried too hard. We picked one location, one time of day, and enough margin for the pair to stop thinking about the camera after the first few minutes.

As the light dropped, the photographs became softer and less posed on their own. Small prompts replaced formal direction, and the wind off the water gave the frames movement that would have been impossible to fake.

The gallery ended up feeling equal parts polished and relaxed, which is usually where the strongest portrait work lives. Good light helps, but trust and patience matter more.
